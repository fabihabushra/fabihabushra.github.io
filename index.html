The thrill of seeing my robot do what I envisioned it to do is very fulfilling to me. It's these little pieces of joy that drive me to learn new things.

Research Inspiration

My journey started with pre-programmed robots. I started imagining what if these robots could be more. I did not have direct exposure to AI robotics since it is an unexplored area of research in my part of the world. But the thought of an assistant robot led me to backtrack to the research being conducted in other places of the world. I began with deep learning and did a computer vision project.

But I realized that the project had certain limitations which are concerned with the data-driven approach of the model. After much digging, I came to understand that the current breakthroughs in AI are a function of time and computational resources. That's why the current robots are only capable of doing repetitive tasks in controlled environments. But they do not have the "commonsense reasoning" ability to reliably operate in a human-unstructured environment and collaborate with us. This perspective leads me to my current research interests which are:

  • Robot Learning
  • Deep Reinforcement Learning
  • Human-robot Interaction

    • Key topics of interest: hierarchical learning, meta-reinforcement learning, inverse reinforcement learning and unsupervised reinforcement learning.

    Looking ahead:
  • What approach will bootstrap the robot-learning process to be sample-efficient and application-agnostic?
